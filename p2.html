Design Process

The design process for this one was very different than the first assignment; it was more experimental. I first drew my face on Procreate. My face is based off of Slipknot's Corey Taylor's mask from their "All Hope Is Gone" album.

I knew from the moment I drew the sketch, that I wanted to give the final assignment a look and feel of horror and uneasiness. I took simple shapes (rounded rectangles and circles) and simplified the initial sketch. Originally, I tried to create bezier curves to have a more "face-like" shape, but I couldn't make that work.

Once I had the basic elements of the mask coded, I wanted to add mousePressed elements to generator different sized faces. From there, that's where things got experimental. I referenced the "blinking" code from user "doubleshow." This created a strobe effect for the eyes, mouth, and nose, similar to the strobing lights the band uses during their live shows. I took this a step further to create a (mouseX,mouseY) movement to showcase a "3D" like effect. It's like the face is SCREAMING. Finally, I used mouseIfPressed so it generates randomly-sized faces. It also changes the color by using random(255). Overall, the code translated well into the overall idea I initially sketched up. It even managed to have a very "glitchy" and horror vibe to it.

Link to the code: https://editor.p5js.org/kvnelvn/sketches/Hz4b4lemX

What the hell did I discover?

Face Generator was one hell of an assignment. I will not lie, I did actually enjoy this one. Maybe it's because I experimented and looked for different ways to code new things. It also taught me a few new coding skills, of which I hope to retain and grow from. This assignment got me to understand the constraints that we can incorporate into our code. Using "if/else" and variables and parameters, I got to code a way to interact with a bunch of randomly generated faces. Perhaps I spent too much time experimenting, because the final outcome did not really reflect my initial min/max sketch of the mask. But it's definitely an interesting journey to go from the sketch to code. In the end, I was still able to generate a variety of different shapes. Perhaps I need to constrain myself next time (get it?) and focus more on nailing my concepts. Or do I just say, fuck it, and design from my heart?
