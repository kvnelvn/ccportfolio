Lost & Found

Description of Camille's Object

My dog's frisbee. A circular off-white sphere lost in what is most likely a field of grass. It has a raised lip that gives it grip. There are three smaller circles with a lighter stroke than the outline. They are centered evenly within each other and very close to one another so they barely overlap. The inner circle has enough room for an outline of a bone in the middle. The frisbee is a medium size (average frisbee size), and has an even texture.

Design Process

As a first time coder, I'm not going to lie: this was an extremely difficult process for me. I come from a design and art background, so to draw an image through code felt unnatural. However, I tried to treat this process how I would with Photoshop or Illustrator. I applied concepts from these applications to the assignment; organized by layers, group objects together and applied different setting to them, and so on. Instead of shapes and objects that I can create with my trackpack, I had to create them through another language. Designing with this mindset definitely helped me complete the assignment.

The process I took was utilizing basic shapes to create Camille's lost object. Her description definitely helped in doing this, because she does mention "sphere" and "circle." Going forward, I used primarily circles and triangles to draw up the frisbee, and the grass that it was probably lost in. Even the dog bone was coded together using four circles, a rectangle, and two lines. Though, I will admit, it was difficult to do an outline versus a "fill" for the bone.

Reflection

Overall, Camille used very basic language to describe her object. She utilized familiar shapes, like sphere and circle, to describe the contents of the frisbee. Not only did she provide decriptions of shapes, but she also gave spacial descriptions, so I knew where to put the overlapping circles and bone. As a result, I interpreted the image quite literally, because it was a very literal description. There was not a lot of room for creative freedom, which I did not mind! The one aspect of the description I had difficulty grasping was "raised lip." That description threw me off, because it was difficult to code that without it ending up unlike the shape and image of a frisbee. As a result, I ended up creating a shadow on the frisbee to give it the illusion that it was not a flat circle, and had an area that was able to be gripped.

The coded image turned out almost what I initially sketched out. I knew I wanted to depict it at an angle, so it wasn't just a flat 2D circle. The addition of the shadows, and the "shear" of the shapes helped bring the 3D aspect of it alive. Interpreting it through sketching and code could not have been more different. When you have a pencil and paper, you can easily and quickly draw the image of a frisbee, shade it to give it the 3D effect. In code, this is not a simple process (for me at least). I had to think about the coordinates of where the shapes would land, and connect pixels in order to get rid of gaps between shapes. Don't even get me started on "rotate." Overall, I think this is a successful interpretation of Camille's lost object.
