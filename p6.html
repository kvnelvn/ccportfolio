Time Goes By . . . So Slowly

I think I check my email too many times during the day. Not only do I have my school email connected to my phone, but I also have my work email as well. Why is it that on my days-off, I continue to check my work email? And why is it that on days I do not have school, I continue to check my school email? Do better, Kevin.

The Design In Question

So, the amount of times I checked my email for the past week was quite a lot. The first thing I knew I had to do was figure out a format of how I would do the arrays. Knowing that I had both time and date, I set my time arrays under defined days. After I had the arrays down, I went straight to the design of it.

I was inspired by the time assignment from a few weeks back. Seeing as I was keeping track of different times, I wanted to represent the data through a clock visualization. I used nested forLoop and arrays to create 10 clocks per day, for seven days. Each clock is a 24hr clock as opposed to 12hr; so where the 6hr time is on a standard clock is the 12hr time on my clock.

Lastly, I represented each hand of the clock with different colors to represent the different days as well. As an added bonus, I added a color animation based on a sin() and cos() map so the clock's colors are constantly changing based on the RGB codes.
