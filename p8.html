Predator Cam

The experiemental camera that keeps on giving.

The "Infrared" Camera

No, it's not really infrared. But I knew I wanted to, visually, mimic the way infrared cameras capture their subjects. Cameras can capture many different subjects through many different forms. For me, I was inspired by infrared/thermal lenses, specifically from the film "Predator." I was watching how the Predator uses the infrared/thermal vision to hunt its prey using their heat signatures to identify them. What if infrared cameras were used in a surveillance context, or interrogation context, or some other CSI context? An infrared camera can spot heat signatures, and therefore identify things that a regular camera wouldn't have seen otherwise. Is there an inherently "good" or "bad" use of this type of camera? To me, that's the meaning I'm trying to impart through this assignment and experimental camera. Even though it's not actually a themral camera, it's still interesting to see it in this context.

How it's made:

First of all, big shoutout to Sean for helping me through this assignment. I was quite lost, but Sean's fantastic guidance helped me get to where I am with this experimental camera. King.

Once I understood the context of "capture" and arrays, using the pixels, I knew I had to separate out the RGB pixels in order to then assign a shape and fill to each one. Using if/else statements, I did just that. I separated the values (0, 255) and assigned a specific if statement to them. For example, if any of the RGB pixels were less than 50 in value, then it would generate a specific fill color and shape. I did this on three separate if/else statements to generate an infrared look.

Instead of running my for loops under x & y++, I did a step count of 10 so that I could render the camera capture easier. Additionally, it made it possibile to code different shapes for the if/else statements. I did square, circle, and triangles for each of the if/else statements so that there was a variety within the canvas. I also wanted to give the camera a feeling that it's "alive" so I made some of the colors and shape sizes random(), so that it would fluctutate and almost feel like it's "breathing" - something to mimic and reflect the nature of heat signatures and body movements.

As I reflect...

I think about the access that our cameras have to, well everything. The very computer I did this assignment on was on and running the entire time; my iPhone's camera is used to unlock the phone; where I work has security cameras posted up on walls and ceilings. It's interesting to think about the reliance, but also the rejection, of camera technology that's increasingly embedded in our lives. I'm neither 100% for or against this. Sometimes I love cameras - I love being able to capture selfies with my friends, or to try out fun unique filters done by creative coders. But I also dislike that there's always the possibility that someone could access my camera and watch me, without my knowledge of it. So who knows? I certainly don't.
